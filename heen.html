Hind's Higher Education Network

هيّن· HIEN

From the Arabic هيّن — gentle, attainable, within reach — and honouring Hind.

Higher education, back within reach — for every displaced student.

HIEN is a global network of academics, universities and volunteers working together to restore access to higher education for students in Gaza whose studies have been interrupted by war. Open‑access courses, devices, mentorship, and a community that refuses to let their futures be erased.

The gap we are closing

Every university in Gaza has been destroyed or severely damaged.

A generation of young scholars — medical students, engineers, teachers, researchers — have had their lecture halls turned to rubble, their libraries burned, their laptops lost. Many continue to study in tents, sharing a single phone between classmates, writing exam answers under bombardment.

HIEN was built for them. Not as charity, but as solidarity — a bridge between the world's universities and the students who refuse to give up. We provide devices, curated open‑access courses, one‑to‑one academic mentorship, and recognised pathways that keep their degrees moving forward.

"Education is the one thing they cannot take — if we keep it alive."

How HIEN works

Four steps. One bridge.

A simple, dignified model connecting academics and students — across borders, across disciplines, across the rubble.

01

We register students

Students in Gaza apply through our local coordinators. We verify their enrolment status and academic goals, then match them to the right programme track.

02

We equip them

Every matched student receives a laptop, study stipend and connectivity support — the baseline tools needed to re‑enter academic life from a tent or temporary shelter.

03

Academics step in

Volunteer professors and postgraduates deliver open‑access lectures, one‑to‑one mentorship, and asynchronous seminars in medicine, engineering, humanities and law.

04

Degrees keep moving

We partner with universities to accredit coursework, supervise theses, and create pathways so students do not lose years — or their place in the world of scholarship.

What we offer

A full learning lifeline.

Education is more than access to lectures. HIEN wraps around the student — hardware, human, academic.

Devices & connectivity

Every student gets the baseline tools to study — no matter how many times they have been displaced.

  • Laptop with preloaded resources
  • Internet data bundle
  • Solar charging where possible
  • Technical support line

Academic mentorship

One‑to‑one pairing with faculty and postgraduate scholars in the student's discipline — medicine, engineering, law, humanities.

  • Weekly 1:1 sessions
  • Thesis & research supervision
  • Peer study circles
  • Career & scholarship guidance

Open‑access curriculum

A curated, discipline‑specific library of courses, textbooks and recorded lectures — working offline when needed.

  • Curated open courseware
  • Recorded seminars
  • Offline‑first materials
  • Arabic & English tracks

Good questions

Things people ask about HIEN.

Who is eligible to receive support through HIEN?
HIEN supports university students in Gaza whose higher education has been interrupted by the ongoing war. Priority is given to students in the final years of their degree, students in medicine and the health sciences, and students without access to any device. All applicants are verified through local coordinators working on the ground.
What does the £300 laptop sponsorship cover exactly?
It covers a refurbished laptop preloaded with offline study materials, a 12‑month data/connectivity bundle, basic technical support, and placement into HIEN's mentorship pairing — meaning your sponsorship does not end at the device. 100% of the sponsorship is ring‑fenced for this purpose.
I'm an academic — do I need to commit a lot of time?
No. Mentors typically commit 2–4 hours a month. You choose the shape of your involvement — weekly check‑ins, thesis supervision, occasional seminars, or asynchronous feedback on written work. Whatever you can offer, a student is waiting for it.
How are HIEN students' degrees recognised?
We partner with universities willing to certify coursework, supervise theses, and in some cases offer formal scholarships or visiting places. For students whose home university is still operating remotely, HIEN provides the academic continuity and supplementary mentorship to keep the degree on track.
Is my donation tax‑deductible?
Mothers of Hind is a UK non‑profit company limited by guarantee. UK donors may be eligible for Gift Aid on donations. Donors in other jurisdictions should consult their local tax advisor. For any documentation or receipt requests, contact our team.
How do I know my sponsorship reached a student?
Each sponsored student receives a welcome from the programme and, where safety allows, sponsors are sent a brief update from the programme team. We protect the privacy of students at all times — names and images are only ever shared with explicit consent.
