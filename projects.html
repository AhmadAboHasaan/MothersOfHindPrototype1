01 · Education

Empowering the next generation through learning.

Mothers of Hind is working to rebuild education in Gaza across two complementary initiatives — from foundational primary schooling to higher‑education continuity.

01

The Mothers of Hind School Series

Makeshift primary schools · Gaza · Flagship programme

Through this initiative, MoH establishes makeshift schools for primary‑stage students — equipped with physical classrooms, desks and textbooks — ensuring children once again have access to foundational education.

Recognising that education in Gaza must not ignore the trauma of the past 15 months, MoH offers comprehensive support beyond academics. This includes hot meals, winter clothing, and on‑site medical care through a dedicated nurse and psychotherapist.

Understanding the profound trauma that students carry as they return to school in the aftermath of genocide, we are committed to creating a safe, nurturing space that addresses both their educational and psychological well‑being.

02

Hind's Higher Education Network — هيّن

HIEN · Global academic network · Open‑access platform

MoH is uniting a global network of academics to provide expertise, teaching materials and recorded lectures for students across all higher‑education disciplines.

This initiative seeks to bridge the severe educational gaps caused by the genocide, ensuring students in Gaza have access to high‑quality learning resources despite the destruction of universities and ongoing disruptions to their studies.

In close collaboration with Higher Education authorities in Gaza, we are developing an extensive online platform to serve as a central repository of academic knowledge — offering tailored support to both students and educators in war‑affected regions.

The platform will not only provide course materials but also facilitate mentorship, research collaboration and academic networking, helping to sustain and rebuild the intellectual and professional aspirations of Gaza's youth. By leveraging global academic solidarity, MoH aims to create the largest open‑access database of higher‑education resources dedicated to supporting those whose education has been forcibly interrupted by conflict.

02 · Psychosocial Support

Restoring hope through care.

Physical rehabilitation receives urgent attention, but the psychological toll demands equal urgency. We are building the infrastructure to address it.

01

A psychotherapist network for Gaza

After‑school hubs · External specialists · Capacity‑building

While the world focuses on physical rehabilitation, the psychological toll demands just as much urgency. Both adults and children face severe anxiety, suicidal thoughts and regressive behaviours.

International organisations estimate 22,500+ individuals in Gaza now live with life‑altering injuries (WHO), and the mental‑health challenges go deeper still (ReliefWeb).

Mothers of Hind is addressing the psychosocial crisis in Gaza by establishing a psychotherapist network connecting external specialists with those providing care on the ground. Building on the Mothers of Hind School Series, after school hours we transform school premises into hubs for psychological support — where psychotherapists can receive training and access internet connectivity to strengthen their capacity.

Adult & child therapy

Trauma‑informed support for families, educators and frontline workers in Gaza.

Capacity building

Training and peer‑supervision for local psychotherapists, via secure online connections.

Safe evening hubs

Classrooms become safe therapy spaces after school hours — reusing infrastructure efficiently.

03 · Advocacy & Awareness

Raising voices for those who can't be heard.

We amplify the voices of those affected by war, bringing their stories to light and advocating for meaningful change.

Advocacy is not an afterthought — it is the foundation on which all our other programmes stand. Without awareness, there is no solidarity. Without solidarity, there is no rebuilding.

We organise campaigns to raise awareness about the impact of conflict, and collaborate with media outlets to highlight stories of resilience and survival — always in partnership with the people whose stories we tell.

Strategic campaigns about the impact of conflict on children and families.
